Overview of Marijuana Law

There is both a federal marijuana law and state marijuana law. The Controlled Substances Act (CSA) and the Cole Memos are the foundation of federal marijuana law. The Oregon Medical Marijuana Act (OMMA) and Ballot Measure 91 (2014) are the two major state marijuana laws of Oregon.

Controlled Substances Act

The CSA regulates the manufacture, distribution, possession and use of controlled substances. The CSA creates five Schedules to classify controlled substances; Schedule I is the most controlled and Schedule V is the least.

Schedule I drugs have a high potential for abuse and no currently accepted medical use in the United States. The CSA lists marijuana as a Schedule I drug. Other Schedule I drugs include heroin and LSD.

Cole Memos

The Cole Memos are a series of memos written by Deputy Attorney General James M. Cole. They provide guidance to federal prosecutors on the enforcement of the CSA with the passage of medical or recreational marijuana legalization in some states.
